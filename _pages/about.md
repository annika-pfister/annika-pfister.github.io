I am an Electrical & Computer Engineering PhD student in the [Ingraham Lab](https://kim-ingraham.com/) at the University of Washington, Seattle. My research interests include rehabilitation robotics, user preference, and user-collaborative engineering. My current research is focused on transcutaneous spinal cord stimulation and exoskeletons for walking rehabilitation for adults with spinal cord injury.

I am also a student member of the Center for Research and Education on Accessible Technology and Experiences ([CREATE](https://create.uw.edu/)) at UW, and love getting involved in accessibility-focused outreach through [HuskyADAPT](https://www.huskyadapt.me.uw.edu/) and [GoBabyGo](https://gobabygo.rehab.washington.edu/)!
